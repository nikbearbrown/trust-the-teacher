**Sentence (line 165):** *"The MET project's $45 million investment across seven districts ([Kane et al. 2013](https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED540959.pdf)) demonstrated that even the best composite of multiple measures has measurement error large enough that proponents wished it were not."*

- **Source:** [Gates Foundation MET press release 2013](https://www.gatesfoundation.org/ideas/media-center/press-releases/2013/01/measures-of-effective-teaching-project-releases-final-research-report); [USP Gates resource page](https://usprogram.gatesfoundation.org/news-and-insights/articles/measures-of-effective-teaching-project)

## Single Sharpest Factual Problem

**The Hattie "psychological safety at *d* ≈ 0.44" claim on line 21.** This is the single load-bearing empirical claim in the Measure 1 section that is straightforwardly wrong on the label. Hattie's database lists **classroom cohesion** at 0.44, not psychological safety. The two are conceptually related but not interchangeable in the literature — psychological safety as a construct comes from Edmondson's organizational behavior work, not from the school-effects meta-analytic tradition Hattie synthesizes. A reader who follows the link to visiblelearningmetax.com and searches for "psychological safety" will find no such entry. The fix is one of two: relabel to "classroom cohesion" (the actual Hattie influence at 0.44) or cite a different source for psychological safety as a school construct. This is more important to fix than the $45M/$52M MET figure because the MET dollar amount is variably reported in secondary sources and the chapter's $45M is at least defensible from some sources; the Hattie label is not defensible from the cited source.
