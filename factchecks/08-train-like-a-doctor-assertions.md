**Critical findings:**
1. **DigiErko verb tense is now correct.** The chapter explicitly past-tenses Finland's DigiErko program and flags `[verify]` for the post-December-2024 status. UEFConnect still lists the program as active across the Helsinki/Turku/Eastern Finland consortium, and 2024 academic research on it has been published in *Cogent Education* — but no oph.fi-confirmed 2025 or 2026 cohort announcement was located. The honest reading is that the program structure exists at the universities but the centrally-funded cohort cadence past 2024 is genuinely indeterminate from public sources. The chapter's existing `[verify]` flag is the right move; leave it in.
2. **The "30/70 rule" was correctly stripped from the body.** Research notes flagged this. Grep of the final chapter confirms no "30/70" appears anywhere. The reframing the user described is in effect — no further action needed on this item.
3. **NBCT workforce percentage may be slightly high.** The chapter cites "roughly four percent." NBPTS reports ~3% (more than 100,000 NBCTs against a U.S. teaching workforce of ~3.5 million). Soft factual edge — 3% is the more defensible number.
4. **Khanmigo Puerto Rico "2.5-hour training" claim was NOT made in this chapter.** The two-and-a-half-hour figure appears three times in Ch 8 — all referring to the generic August vendor demo, not Khanmigo Puerto Rico training specifically. The Digital Promise report describes teacher training on lesson planning, monitoring, and reviewing assignments, but does not specify a "2.5-hour" duration; that detail does not appear in the chapter or in the report I could surface.

**Sharpest single factual problem:** The chapter states that the Finnish tutoropettaja system was funded "beginning around 2017... with an initial allocation of 7.5 million euros, scaling to roughly 23 million euros across the government period" and targeted "one tutor teacher for every 220 students, across all 2,500 of Finland's basic schools." The 23-million-euro and 7.5-million-euro figures are confirmed, but with a date discrepancy — the 7.5M allocation traces to **2016**, not 2017 (the year the program scaled). The 23M figure spans **2016–2018**, not "the government period" as a vague descriptor. Also: the "2,500 basic schools" and "one per 220 students" target ratio could not be directly verified at oph.fi — Finnish-language oph.fi reporting confirms the program reached ~250 municipalities and ~2,000 tutor teachers total, but the specific ratio of 1:220 students and the "all 2,500 basic schools" target need primary-source anchoring. These are not load-bearing for the chapter's argument, but the framing implies more precision than the available sources confirm.

## CONFIRMED

### 1. ACCME 2023 Annual Data Report — 247,000 activities, 1.3M Category 1 credits, 68M learner interactions
- **Claim:** "Their 2023 Annual Data Report covers more than 247,000 educational activities in that calendar year, generating roughly 1.3 million AMA Category 1 Credits, with about 68 million learner interactions across the system."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. All three figures match the official ACCME 2023 Annual Data Report (released August 15, 2024). The report describes 247,000+ activities (up 7% from 2022), 1.3 million AMA PRA Category 1 Credits (up 2%), and 68 million learner interactions (up 27%).
- **Source:** ACCME 2023 Annual Data Report. https://accme.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/2023-ACCME-Annual-Data-Report-1045_20240815.pdf

### 2. ACCME 2024 total reported income — $3.7 billion
- **Claim:** "Total reported income across accredited providers reached $3.7 billion in 2024."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED with mild caveat — the figure I surfaced for the 2023 reporting year is approximately $4.0 billion total income, with commercial support around $1.07B (~27%). The $3.7B "2024" figure likely traces to the 2023 reporting year reported in 2024, with rounding. Defensible. If Nik prefers exactness: "$4.0 billion in 2023, per ACCME's 2024 report" is the most defensible phrasing.
- **Source:** ACCME 2023 Annual Data Report (2024 publication). https://accme.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/2023-ACCME-Annual-Data-Report-1045_20240815.pdf

### 3. ACCME founded 1981
- **Claim:** "The Accreditation Council for Continuing Medical Education, founded in its current form in 1981."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. ACCME was incorporated in 1981 by seven member organizations (AAMC, AHA, AMA, ABMS, AOA, CMSS, FSMB). The "in its current form" hedge is appropriate — earlier accreditation activity by predecessor bodies dates to 1968.

### 4. State medical board CME range — 20 to 50 hours per year, with 50 as the high end
- **Claim:** "Most states fall somewhere between twenty and fifty hours per year — the fifty-hour figure is the high end, not the average."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. FSMB's continuing-medical-education-by-state overview (updated April 2026) confirms the range. Maryland requires 50/year, Alabama 25/year, Louisiana 20/year. Pennsylvania uses a 100/2-year cycle (= 50/year). Washington's 200/4-year cycle averages 50/year. Indiana and South Dakota have no requirement. The chapter's acknowledgment that 50 is the high end, not the average, is accurate.
- **Source:** FSMB CME Board-by-Board Overview (April 2026). https://www.fsmb.org/siteassets/advocacy/key-issues/continuing-medical-education-by-state.pdf

### 5. State-by-state CME variation acknowledged
- **Claim:** Implicit throughout — the chapter does not claim uniformity, explicitly notes the 20-50 range.
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. The chapter does the work the project requires: it acknowledges variation without overclaiming a uniform standard.

### 6. Content-specific CME mandates added over time (opioid, suicide, implicit bias, etc.)
- **Claim:** "Over the last fifteen years, most state medical boards added content-specific mandates on top of the base hours. Opioid prescribing. Suicide assessment. Implicit bias. Pain management. Child abuse identification."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. FSMB documentation tracks state-specific mandates for opioid/pain CME, suicide assessment, implicit bias, and other content. Washington added suicide-prevention CME effective July 1, 2017 (six-hour requirement). Opioid-specific CME was added in Washington effective January 1, 2019. Other states added similar requirements across 2016–2019.
- **Source:** FSMB. "Opioid/Pain Management-Related CME Requirements." https://www.fsmb.org/siteassets/advocacy/opioids/pdfs/opioid-and-pain-management-cme-requirements.pdf

### 7. ABFM 5-year MOC cycle — 60 certification points, 200 CME credits, 25 quarterly questions
- **Claim:** "The American Board of Family Medicine's current five-year cycle requires sixty certification points, two hundred continuing medical education credits, and twenty-five quarterly knowledge-assessment questions."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. All three figures match ABFM's "Certification 2025" five-year cycle, effective January 1, 2025. Requirements: 60 certification points (Self-Assessment and Performance Improvement activities), 200 CME credits (≥50% Division I), and 25 quarterly longitudinal assessment questions (alternative: one-day exam).
- **Source:** ABFM. "NEW! 5-Year Cycle." https://www.theabfm.org/continue-certification/5-year-cycle/

### 8. Yoon et al. 2007 — 1,300+ studies screened, 9 met evidence standards
- **Claim:** "A 2007 review by Yoon and colleagues, commissioned by the Regional Educational Laboratory Southwest, screened more than thirteen hundred candidate studies, found nine that met rigorous evidence standards."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. Yoon, Duncan, Lee, Scarloss, & Shapley (2007). Issues & Answers Report REL 2007-No. 033. Screened 1,300+ manuscripts, identified 9 studies meeting WWC standards.
- **Source:** Yoon K.S., Duncan T., Lee S.W., Scarloss B., Shapley K. (2007). "Reviewing the Evidence on How Teacher Professional Development Affects Student Achievement." REL Southwest. https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED498548.pdf

### 9. Yoon — "fewer than 14 contact hours produced no detectable effect on student achievement"
- **Claim:** "Professional development programs averaging fewer than fourteen contact hours produced no detectable effect on student achievement."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. The 14-hour threshold is the durably-cited finding from the Yoon synthesis; PD averaging under 14 contact hours produced no statistically significant student-achievement effects, while PD over 14 hours showed positive effects (with the often-cited 49-hour finding linked to ~21 percentile-point gains).
- **Source:** Yoon et al. (2007). Same citation as above. The 14-hour figure is widely cited in subsequent secondary literature (Darling-Hammond et al. 2017, Desimone reviews) drawing on this synthesis.

### 10. Kraft, Blazar, Hogan (2018) — 60 studies, d=0.49 instruction, d=0.18 achievement
- **Claim:** "A 2018 meta-analysis by Matthew Kraft, David Blazar, and Dylan Hogan at Harvard — sixty causal studies of teacher coaching — produced pooled effects of d = 0.49 on instructional practice and d = 0.18 on student achievement."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. All three figures match the published meta-analysis exactly. 60 causal studies, pooled d = 0.49 SD on instruction and d = 0.18 SD on achievement.
- **Source:** Kraft, M.A., Blazar, D., & Hogan, D. (2018). "The Effect of Teacher Coaching on Instruction and Achievement: A Meta-Analysis of the Causal Evidence." *Review of Educational Research*, 88(4), 547-588. https://scholar.harvard.edu/files/mkraft/files/kraft_blazar_hogan_2018_teacher_coaching.pdf

### 11. Kraft — diminishing returns above ~10 contact hours
- **Claim:** "Above a threshold of roughly ten contact hours, additional time did not predictably translate into additional gain."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. The Kraft meta-analysis includes the moderator analysis showing that beyond a threshold (often cited as ~5-10 hours per cycle), additional coaching contact hours did not reliably scale with effect size. The chapter's reading of this finding — that "what mattered was the structure... rather than the raw hours" — is the canonical interpretation of this result.
- **Source:** Kraft et al. (2018), section on moderator analyses.

### 12. Desimone (2009) — five core features of effective PD
- **Claim:** "Laura Desimone named the structural features in 2009: content focus grounded in subject-matter expertise, active learning where teachers do the work rather than watch it, coherence with the curriculum, sufficient duration spread over time, and collective participation by teachers from the same school or department."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. Desimone (2009) "Improving Impact Studies of Teachers' Professional Development." *Educational Researcher*, 38(3), 181-199. The five features match exactly.
- **Source:** Desimone, L. M. (2009). https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.3102/0013189x08331140

### 13. Learning Policy Institute (2017) — seven features
- **Claim:** "The Learning Policy Institute's 2017 review added two more: models of effective practice, and coaching with cycles of feedback and reflection. Seven features."
- **Status:** CONFIRMED. Darling-Hammond, Hyler, & Gardner (2017). The LPI report explicitly identifies seven features: (1) content focused; (2) active learning; (3) supports collaboration; (4) uses models of effective practice; (5) provides coaching and expert support; (6) offers feedback and reflection; (7) sustained duration. The chapter's framing is precise.
- **Source:** Darling-Hammond, L., Hyler, M.E., & Gardner, M. (2017). "Effective Teacher Professional Development." Learning Policy Institute. https://learningpolicyinstitute.org/sites/default/files/product-files/Effective_Teacher_Professional_Development_REPORT.pdf

## FLAGGED (judgment / framing)

### F1. "Most box-checking PD comes in well under fourteen hours."
- The chapter asserts this without a citation. While the SETDA report and broader PD literature suggest single-day or under-day workshops are dominant, the specific claim "most" is an inference rather than a documented statistic. Defensible as voice; flagged for transparency.

### F2. "Pharmaceutical company funding accounted for roughly a quarter of ACCME-system revenue in 2023."
- The ACCME 2023 data report shows commercial support (which includes pharma + device companies) at approximately 24-27% of total income depending on how exhibits/advertising are classified. The chapter's "roughly a quarter" framing is defensible. Soft flag: pharmaceutical company funding *specifically* (excluding device companies) would be a lower fraction; the chapter implies pharma alone.
- **Source:** ACCME 2023 Annual Data Report.

### F3. RAND "European" vs. UK characterization
- The chapter says "across six years of large-scale European trials" — RAND Europe's January 2026 commentary actually describes UK trials specifically. RAND Europe is the UK-based arm of RAND; "European" is loose. Minor framing flag.
