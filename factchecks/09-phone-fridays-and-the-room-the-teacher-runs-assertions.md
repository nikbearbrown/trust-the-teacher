**Sharpest problem:** The Colorado opening scene presents a fictional classroom practice as if it were the state law's mandate. HB25-1135 requires *districts* to *adopt a policy* by July 1, 2026 — it does NOT require devices in lockers Mon–Thu with Friday tablet time. ## Critical / Immediate Review

### CRITICAL #1 — Colorado opening scene mis-frames what the state actually required

**Sentence (line 9):** *"Monday through Thursday, the tablets stay in the lockers from first bell to last, part of a policy the school adopted when Colorado's legislature required every public school in the state to have one."*

- **Verdict:** OUTDATED / MISFRAMED. The Colorado statute (HB25-1135, signed by Governor Polis 2025) does **not** require "every public school" to do anything operational with phones. It requires the Colorado School for the Deaf and Blind, each institute charter school, each district charter school, and each local board of education to **adopt, implement, and post a policy concerning student communication device possession and use during the school day by July 1, 2026**. It explicitly leaves the content of the policy to local boards. The Front Porch / KUNC / Vail Daily coverage all stress that this is a "districts must have a policy" law, not a statewide ban or a state-imposed daily structure.
- **Concern:** The opening scene describes a particular practice (lockers Mon–Thu, devices Fri) as if it were the law's mandate. A careful reader who looks up HB25-1135 will not find a "tablets in lockers Monday through Thursday" requirement anywhere in it — that is Brown's "Phone Fridays" design pattern projected back onto a Colorado classroom as if the legislature wrote it. Combined with the fact that the chapter never names the Colorado school or teacher, the scene reads as a real example but is actually an unlabeled composite.
- **Recommended action:** Either (a) label the opening as composite / illustrative ("Imagine a Friday morning in a Colorado middle school..."), or (b) keep the scene specific only if you can name a real Colorado school that has actually adopted this Mon–Thu lockers + Fri tablets pattern under HB25-1135. The current phrasing — *"part of a policy the school adopted when Colorado's legislature required every public school in the state to have one"* — implies a causal chain (law → required practice → this teacher's day) that the law does not establish.
- **Source:** [Colorado HB25-1135 bill text & summary](https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/hb25-1135); [CBS Colorado: New law requires Colorado schools to adopt, implement student cellphone policies](https://www.cbsnews.com/colorado/news/new-law-requires-cellphone-policies-colorado-school-districts/); [Vail Daily: Colorado schools roll out stricter cellphone bans](https://www.vaildaily.com/news/colorado-schools-cellphone-bans-deadline/)
