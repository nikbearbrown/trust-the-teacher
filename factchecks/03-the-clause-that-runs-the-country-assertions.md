## Critical / Immediate Review

### CONTRADICTED #1 — "thirty-five state phone ban statutes" / "thirty-five state statutes" (lines 14, 24, 32, 34, 144)

**Sentence (line 14):** *"The phrase is in thirty-five state phone ban statutes."*
**Sentence (line 144):** *"Some version of them appears in thirty-five state statutes."*

- **Verdict:** CONTRADICTED in precise form. As of March 2026, **Ballotpedia counts 33 states** that have enacted statewide K-12 cellphone bans/restrictions (with Kansas as the 33rd, March 5, 2026 — Gov. Mike Braun signed SB 78). A broader Ballotpedia tally of 41 states includes 8 states whose laws "encourage" districts or require a policy without specifying form. The chapter's "thirty-five" sits between these two figures and does not match either canonical count from Ballotpedia's phone-ban tracker.
- **Concern:** The "thirty-five" framing is load-bearing — it appears 4+ times and structures the chapter's central argument ("thirty-five states, six approaches, zero definitions"). The figure may have been current at a previous draft date but is now out of sync with Ballotpedia's published March 2026 count of 33.
- **Source check:** [Ballotpedia — Kansas 33rd state](https://news.ballotpedia.org/2026/03/30/kansas-becomes-the-33rd-state-to-enact-a-k-12-cellphone-ban/); [Ballotpedia tracker](https://ballotpedia.org/State_policies_on_cellphone_use_in_K-12_public_schools)
- **Recommended action:** Reconcile to either (a) "thirty-three states" (Ballotpedia's narrow count of statewide bans/restrictions) or (b) "thirty-five states" with an explicit citation source — and confirm whether the "thirty-five" includes Virginia (executive-order-based guidance, not statute) and other non-statutory states. The chapter explicitly calls them "statutes" — under that test the figure should be the narrower one. Across all four occurrences.

### CONTRADICTED #2 — Indiana SB 78 attribution + sunset clause framing (line 46)

**Sentence (line 46):** *"Indiana, alone among the fifty states, added a sunset clause to its teacher-directed exemption — the provision expires July 1, 2028."*

- **Verdict:** CONFIRMED in substance but **misframed**. Indiana's bell-to-bell statute (SB 78, signed by Gov. Braun in March 2026, effective July 1, 2026) does contain a transition date of July 1, 2028 — but the 2028 date is **not** a "sunset clause" on the teacher-directed exemption. It is a **device-transition** rule: before July 1, 2028, teacher-directed educational use may occur on either a school-supplied or a school-sanctioned (approved & filtered) personal device; after June 30, 2028, instructional use must be on a school-supplied device only. The teacher-directed exemption itself does not sunset. What sunsets is the permission for personal devices to carry teacher-directed work.
- **Concern:** The chapter then says "They did not write what happens when it expires" — this is wrong. The statute does specify what happens (school-supplied devices only).
- **Source:** [Chalkbeat Indiana — bell-to-bell expansion](https://www.chalkbeat.org/indiana/2026/02/25/stricter-school-cellphone-ban-passes/); [Indiana Capital Chronicle](https://indianacapitalchronicle.com/2026/05/13/indiana-schools-face-stricter-cellphone-rules-under-new-bell-to-bell-law-starting-july-1/); [SB 78 enrolled text](https://iga.in.gov/pdf-documents/124/2026/senate/bills/SB0078/SB0078.08.ENRH.pdf)
- **Recommended action:** Either reframe to "Indiana set a July 2028 transition date that requires school-supplied devices to take over from personal devices for teacher-directed educational use" or recharacterize as a *device-source* sunset, not a *teacher-directed exemption* sunset. The "Indiana, alone among the fifty states" claim should also be checked; it is plausible but not directly verified.

### OUTDATED #1 — "Twenty-two states passed K-12 cell phone restrictions in 2025 alone" (line 22)

The "twenty-two" is defensible as a year-2025 snapshot, but readers should note the count grew through Q4 2025 and into early 2026.
- **Source:** [Ballotpedia August 2025: "22 states"](https://news.ballotpedia.org/2025/08/08/twenty-two-states-enacted-k-12-cellphone-bans-so-far-in-2025/); [Ballotpedia November 2025: Wisconsin as 21st of 2025](https://news.ballotpedia.org/2025/11/12/wisconsin-becomes-the-21st-state-to-adopt-a-k-12-cellphone-ban-in-2025/)
