## Critical / Immediate Review

### CRITICAL #1 — Stroebe 2020: wrong journal in the citation (cross-reference from Ch. 10)

**Sentence (line 142 in "Notes" — Chapter 10 entry; and line 277 in "References"):** *"Stroebe, W. (2020). Student Evaluations of Teaching Encourages Poor Teaching. *Educational Psychologist*, 55(3), 153–168."*

- **Verdict:** CONTRADICTED.
- **Issue:** Stroebe's 2020 paper was published in *Basic and Applied Social Psychology* 42(4): 276–294, https://doi.org/10.1080/01973533.2020.1756817 — not in *Educational Psychologist*. The volume/issue/pages currently in both entries (55(3), 153–168) do not match the cited paper. See Ch. 10 fact-check CRITICAL #2 for full discussion.
- **Action required:** Replace both back-matter entries with:
  > Stroebe, W. (2020). "Student Evaluations of Teaching Encourages Poor Teaching and Contributes to Grade Inflation: A Theoretical and Empirical Analysis." *Basic and Applied Social Psychology* 42(4): 276–294. https://doi.org/10.1080/01973533.2020.1756817
- **Suggested reference:** As above.

### CRITICAL #2 — "Irreducibly Human framework, a seven-tier taxonomy": needs reconciliation with public framework structure (five courses)

**Sentence (line 25 in About the Author):** *"He is the architect of the [Irreducibly Human](https://irreducibly.xyz) framework, a seven-tier taxonomy of human cognitive capacities used to distinguish what AI can do, what it can simulate, what it cannot do yet, and what it cannot do because it has no body, no social life, no risk, no mortality, and no stake in the consequences."*

- **Verdict:** FLAGGED (consistency / framework structure).
- **Issue:** The Irreducibly Human public site (irreducibly.xyz) presents the work as a five-course sequence — this is the recurring reconciliation problem flagged in the research notes brief for this fact-check. The book's TIKTOC.md (line 390) refers to "the seven-tier taxonomy of human cognitive capacities." Whether the public-facing five-course structure maps to seven tiers internally, whether the "seven tiers" is a recent expansion that has not yet been reflected on the public site, or whether the two framings are different artifacts that should be cited separately, needs author resolution. CLAUDE.md §6 ("Name papers, people, products, institutions") and §7 hard rule 1 (no fabricated specifics) both push toward making the structure verifiable.
- **Action required:** Author should (a) update irreducibly.xyz to surface the seven-tier taxonomy explicitly if it exists; (b) revise the back-matter claim to "the Irreducibly Human framework, a taxonomy of human cognitive capacities currently developed as a five-course sequence" if the seven-tier framing is still in draft; or (c) add a footnote explaining the relationship. The current state — an external link to a site that presents five courses, alongside a book claim of seven tiers — is the kind of small inconsistency a hostile reviewer would surface.
- **Suggested reference:** Internal — author decision.

### SPECIALIST — UNVERIFIED
**Assertion type:** BASIC
**Sentence (lines 311, 313, 317, 319 in Glossary):** Glossary entries for *AI+1 teacher*, *cognitive offloading*, *the fluency trap*, *implementation gap*.
**Claim checked:** Empirical / definitional accuracy of the four glossary entries that make load-bearing empirical claims.
**Findings:**
- **AI+1 teacher** (line 311): Internally consistent with the chapters. Glossary correctly contrasts with the Bastani 2025 "AI replaces teacher" deployment pattern — though Bastani 2025 did not test an "AI replaces teacher" condition exactly; it tested GPT Base (unguided student access) vs. GPT Tutor (scaffolded) vs. control. The "AI replaces teacher" framing is the chapter's editorial reading of the GPT Base condition, not the paper's framing. Acceptable but worth softening to "the unguided-AI deployment pattern" rather than "AI replaces teacher."
- **Cognitive offloading** (line 313): Definition matches Risko & Gilbert (2016). Citation correct. No flag.
- **The fluency trap** (line 317): Defines a real mechanism and attributes it correctly to Carpenter et al. 2013 and Bjork & Bjork 1992. The specific term "fluency trap" appears to be the book's coinage rather than a literature standard term (see Frontmatter fact-check) — the glossary handles attribution honestly by anchoring the *mechanism* to the established literature without claiming the term itself is established. Acceptable.
- **Implementation gap** (line 319): Definition is consistent with the implementation-science literature (Pressman & Wildavsky 1973 originated the term; Larry Cuban 1986 *Teachers and Machines* established the EdTech-specific pattern). Citation is absent; light flag.
**Expert review needed:** Light — soften the AI+1 entry on "AI replaces teacher" framing; consider adding Cuban 1986 / Pressman & Wildavsky 1973 to the implementation-gap entry.

**Claim checked:** Sanskrit etymology of Medhavi (मेधावी).
**Finding:** Confirmed in substance. *Medhāvī* (मेधावी) derives from Sanskrit *medhā* (मेधा), meaning intellect / intelligence / mental vigor; the adjectival/nominal form *medhāvī* is glossed in standard Sanskrit lexicons (Monier-Williams) as "intelligent, wise, learned." The product brand spelling varies between *medhavy.substack.com* / *medhavy.com* (line 25, line 305) and *medhavi* / *Medhavi* (lines 299, 303). Minor brand-spelling inconsistency.
**Expert review needed:** Light — pick a consistent transliteration (Medhavi vs Medhavy) and use it throughout.
