## Critical / Immediate Review

### OUTDATED #1 — State count framing

**Sentence (line 5):** *"Twenty-two states passed some version of it in 2025 alone."*

- **Verdict:** OUTDATED in surrounding context — section header later says "Thirty-three states" (line 37).
- **Reason:** Both numbers are individually defensible (22 enactments *in calendar 2025*; 33 total states with bans as of early 2026, after Kansas became #33 in March 2026 per Ballotpedia). But the chapter does not reconcile them. A reader who sees "22 states passed it in 2025" on line 5 and "Thirty-three states" header on line 37 will be confused; the gap is the pre-2025 states plus the 2026 entrants (Kansas was 5th of 2026, per Ballotpedia News 2026-03-30). Recommend adding a clause like "plus eleven previously and into 2026" or restating the 33 figure with cleaner attribution.
- **Source:** [Ballotpedia: Kansas becomes 33rd state](https://news.ballotpedia.org/2026/03/30/kansas-becomes-the-33rd-state-to-enact-a-k-12-cellphone-ban/); [Ballotpedia: Twenty-two states enacted bans in 2025](https://news.ballotpedia.org/2025/08/08/twenty-two-states-enacted-k-12-cellphone-bans-so-far-in-2025/)

### COMBINATION #1 — Figlio & Özek 16% claim and "30% for Black boys" framing

**Sentence (line 7):** *"The suspension numbers for Black students in that Florida district rose sixteen percent in year one."*

- **Verdict:** CONFIRMED for the 16% figure as stated.
- **Cross-reference (line 171, author's own caveat):** chapter footer flags that *"a figure of approximately thirty percent for Black boys specifically has circulated in press coverage, sharper than the sixteen percent for Black students overall reported in the NBER digest, and the chapter's opening should not overshoot the paper."*
- **Status:** The chapter's main text correctly uses the 16% figure that the NBER Digest reports. The Hechinger Report and other secondary coverage have circulated a higher race-by-sex disaggregation. Author has flagged this for verification against the published tables. Direct verification of the 30% disaggregation against the NBER WP PDF is recommended before any future revision pulls that figure into the body. **Per author note, leave 16% in main text; flag the 30% caveat as in-progress verification.**
- **Source:** [NBER WP 34388](https://www.nber.org/papers/w34388); [NBER Digest 202512](https://www.nber.org/digest/202512/school-cell-phone-bans-and-student-achievement); [Hechinger Report on cellphone bans](https://hechingerreport.org/proof-points-cellphone-bans/)
