Figure 6.4 — The compounding gap over a ten-year career

Cumulative school weeks recovered by a weekly AI-using teacher vs. a non-using colleague. Six weeks per year compound silently into roughly one and a half school years of additional time by year ten.

Read it this way. Same school, same budget, same AI license on the server. The variable that diverges is whether the teacher was prepared to collect the dividend. The gap is not a finding about AI — it is a finding about training.

Inputs: 5.9 h/week saved among weekly AI-using teachers (Gallup-Walton 2025) × a 36-week school year ≈ 6 recovered school weeks per year. Persistence across a full career is the open empirical question; this is the simple-compounding scenario, not a measured longitudinal panel.
